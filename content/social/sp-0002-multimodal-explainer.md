"Multimodal AI" gets thrown around a lot. Here's the plain-English version:

A text-only model can read an insurance claim *after* someone types it up.
A multimodal model looks at the photo of the damage, reads the handwritten
form, cross-checks the policy, and returns a structured assessment — in one
pass, the way a human would.

The modality is just the type of data: text, images, video, audio.

I wrote up what it is, why it matters if you're sitting on visual data, and
where these projects tend to break between the demo and production.
